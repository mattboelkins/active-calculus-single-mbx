Recall that to estimate the value of \(f'(a)\) at a given value of \(x = a\text{,}\) we calculate a difference quotient \(\frac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{h}\) with a relatively small value of \(h\text{.}\) We usually use both positive and negative values of \(h\) in order to account for the behavior of the function on both sides of the point of interest. To that end, we introduce the notion of a central difference and its role in estimating derivatives.
