Consider the alternating geometric series
\begin{equation*} S = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (-1)^k \left( \frac{4}{5} \right)^k = 1 - \frac{4}{5} + \frac{16}{25} - \cdots + (-1)^{n-1} \left( \frac{4}{5} \right)^{n-1} + \cdots\text{.} \end{equation*}
We want to explore how the partial sums of the series compare to and approximate the exact sum of the series.
🔗
  1. Recall that the \(n\)th partial sum, \(S_n\text{,}\) is the sum of the first \(n\) terms of the infinite geometric series \(S\text{.}\) This means that
    \begin{equation*} S_n = \sum_{k=0}^{n-1} (-1)^k \left( \frac{4}{5} \right)^k = 1 - \frac{4}{5} + \frac{16}{25} - \cdots + (-1)^{n-1} \left( \frac{4}{5} \right)^{n-1}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Note that \(S_1 = 1\) and \(S_2 = 1 - \frac{4}{5} = \frac{1}{5} = 0.2\) are provided, and remember that each subsequent partial sum can be found by adding or subtracting one number from the preceding partial sum.
