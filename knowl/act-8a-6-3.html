Activity 8.6.4.

We solve two different initial value problems using power series.

  1. Consider the IVP given by

    \begin{equation*} y' = 2xy, \ y(0) = 1\text{.} \end{equation*}

    1. Let \(y\) be given by the power series

      \begin{equation*} y = c_0 + c_1 x + c_2 x^2 + c_3 x^3 + c_4 x^4 + \cdots + c_n x^n + \cdots\text{.} \end{equation*}

      Find expressions for both \(y'\) and \(2xy\text{.}\)

  2. Consider Airy's equation with initial conditions given by

    \begin{equation*} y'' - xy = 0, \ y(0) = 1, \ y'(0) = 0\text{.} \end{equation*}
