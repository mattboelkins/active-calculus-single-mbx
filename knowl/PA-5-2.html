Preview Activity 5.2.1.

Consider the function \(A\) defined by the rule

\begin{equation*} A(x) = \int_1^x f(t) \, dt\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(f(t) = 4-2t\text{.}\)

  1. Compute \(A(1)\) and \(A(2)\) exactly.

  2. Use the First Fundamental Theorem of Calculus to find a formula for \(A(x)\) that does not involve integrals. That is, use the first FTC to evaluate \(\int_1^x (4-2t) \, dt\text{.}\)

  3. Observe that \(f\) is a linear function; what kind of function is \(A\text{?}\)

  4. Using the formula you found in (b) that does not involve integrals, compute \(A'(x)\text{.}\)

  5. While we have defined \(f\) by the rule \(f(t) = 4-2t\text{,}\) it is equivalent to say that \(f\) is given by the rule \(f(x) = 4 - 2x\text{.}\) What do you observe about the relationship between \(A\) and \(f\text{?}\)
